// Key Findings

What The Data
Reveals

Six confirmed analytical findings across spatial coverage, service frequency, commute burden, modal access, and network connectivity. Two triggers FTA Title VI action.

FINDING 01
Title VI Finding
1.36×
Minority Commute Time Burden
Minority-majority tracts average 28.4 min commute vs 20.9 min for non-minority tracts. The 1.36× disparity exceeds the FTA 1.25× threshold — a formal disparate impact finding under Circular 4702.1B.
FINDING 02
Title VI Finding
1.54×
Hispanic Residents in Transit Deserts
Hispanic residents represent 8.3% of the population in transit desert zones vs 5.4% citywide — a 1.54× overrepresentation that exceeds the FTA threshold. 10.04 sq mi (12.4% of city land) lacks any transit access within a 10-min walk.
FINDING 03
No Title VI Finding
−0.356
The Frequency Paradox
MTA provides more frequent service to vulnerable neighborhoods (r = −0.356, p < 0.001). Q5 residents receive 17.2 min headway vs 25.3 min for Q1 — 8.1 minutes better frequency. The equity gap is not about frequency.
FINDING 04
Core Disparity
+57%
Commute Burden by Quintile
Q5 (most vulnerable) residents average 30.9 min commute vs 19.7 min for Q1 — a 57% gap. Pearson r = +0.605 (p < 0.001), R² = 0.367. Equity score alone explains 36.7% of commute time variation across 199 tracts.
FINDING 05
No Finding
p=.567
Connectivity Equitably Distributed
Routes per stop ranges from 1.46 (Q1) to 1.54 (Q5) — not statistically significant (Welch t-test, p = 0.567). 1,107 transfer points are distributed without equity disparity. Network connectivity is Baltimore's most equitably distributed transit attribute.
FINDING 06
Structural
95.8%
Bus Dependency
95.8% of all 2,678 stops are local bus. Only 21 of 199 tracts (10.5%) have any rail access. Rail serves Metro (21 stops), Light Rail (31 stops), and MARC (5 stops). Baltimore is overwhelmingly bus-dependent — 178 tracts are bus-only.

// Methodology

How The Analysis
Was Built

A 13-stage Python/ArcGIS pipeline combining pedestrian network modeling, GTFS processing, Census demographic analysis, and FTA Title VI compliance testing.

01
GTFS Conversion
Raw GTFS .txt feeds converted to GDB feature classes. 2,678 stops, 433 routes across 5 MTA modes clipped to Baltimore City boundary.
Complete
02
Walk-Time / Service Area
Network Analyst service area solve at 5/10-min walk (3 mph) from all stops. Water bodies erased. OSM network, EPSG 2248.
Complete
03
Census Extraction
ACS 2020 5-year estimates downloaded via Census API. 12 table groups including B02001, B03001, B08013, B17001 for 199 Baltimore tracts.
Complete
04–05
Census Merge & GEOID Fix
All ACS tables joined by GEOID. TRACTCE leading-zero standardization. 11-digit GEOID format enforced across all 199 tracts.
Complete
06–07
Equity Index Build
19-indicator composite score (0–100) via min-max normalization. Equity_Quintile Q1–Q5 assigned. 19 derived demographic percentage fields calculated.
Complete
08
Spatial Gap Analysis
Coverage erased from land boundary → transit desert zones. 12.4% gap identified. Title VI spatial overlay: Hispanic 1.54×.
Complete
09
Desert Demographics
Frequency gap tracts identified (avg headway > 20 min). 18 gap tracts. AM peak headway distribution analyzed by equity quintile.
Complete
10
Travel Time Equity
Pearson r = +0.605, p < 0.001. Q1–Q5 means. Title VI minority commute disparity = 1.36×. The strongest statistical finding in the analysis.
Complete
11
Modal Access Export
Rail classified by mode (Metro/Light Rail/MARC). 57 rail stations, 21 rail-served tracts (10.5%). 95.8% bus dependency confirmed.
Complete
12
Connectivity Analysis
Routes per stop from GTFS. 1,107 transfer points. Welch t-test Q1 vs Q5: p = 0.567 — no disparity. Most equitably distributed attribute.
Complete
13
Frequency Analysis
Per-stop AM peak headways from stop_times.txt. Headway_Min & Freq_Class assigned. r = −0.356 — the Frequency Paradox confirmed.
Complete
14
Job Access Analysis
Job accessibility by transit at 30/45/60-min thresholds. Requires LEHD LODES 2020 block-level employment data from Census OnTheMap.

// Data Sources

Where The Data
Comes From

All data is publicly available and fully reproducible.

01
MTA Maryland GTFS Feeds (2024)
5 feeds: Local Bus, Light Rail, Metro Subway, MARC Train, Commuter Bus. feeds.mta.maryland.gov. 2,678 stops clipped to Baltimore City. 433 routes.
02
US Census ACS 5-Year 2020
12 demographic table groups at Census Tract level for Baltimore City (FIPS 24510). Downloaded via Census API. 199 tracts × 19 equity indicators after processing.
03
OpenStreetMap Road Network (2024)
Pedestrian road network for Baltimore City. Filtered for walkable roads. Projected to EPSG 2248 (StatePlane Maryland). Used as basis for Network Analyst dataset.
04
Baltimore City GIS Open Data
City boundary, land-only polygon, place boundaries. Used for clipping, gap zone calculation, and spatial reference. All layers in EPSG 2248.
05
FTA Circular 4702.1B (2012)
Title VI Program Guidance — Civil Rights Requirements for Federal Transit Recipients. Provides the 1.25× disparate impact threshold applied in all demographic overlay tests.
06
ArcGIS Network Analyst
Service area analysis using Baltimore_Walk_ND. Walking travel mode at 3 mph. 5/10-minute ring polygons from all 2,678 stops. Water bodies erased from results.
